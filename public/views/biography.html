Biography

Brina Simon is a young, energetic trumpet player and music educator based in Los Angeles. She is a member of a number of musical projects including Noble Creatures, The Urban Renewal Project and Noble Horns. She also leads her own group, the Brina Simon Jazz Quartet. Well versed in a wide range of styles from jazz to pop and classical, she can be found playing in clubs, concert halls and pit orchestras across Southern California.

Surrounded by music since birth, she began playing piano at age five. Inspired by her uncle Mathew Simon, a well renowned trumpet player and educator living in Barcelona, she picked up the trumpet at age seven. During her musical education she has studied with some of Los Angelesâ€™ finest jazz musicians including pianist Tamir Hendelman, trombonist Ira Nepus and trumpeter Howie Shear.

In addition to her busy schedule as a performer, Brina Simon maintains a private trumpet and piano studio consisting of students all ages and abilities. She composes music both for her quartet and for area big bands. She has played on a number of recordings, including the Noble Creatures album Strange Behavior, a self-titled album by the L.A. Love Band, and an album by the Los Angeles band Roxford Royals among others. Her studio experience also includes commercial work and recording as a member of several horn sections.

With a profound passion for music and the drive to continually expand as an artist, Brina Simon is excited to share her music and abilities with musicians and audiences alike.
